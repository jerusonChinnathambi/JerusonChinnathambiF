Executive summary

How much should a global brand spend on marketing over the next five years — and where? Using nine years of Red Bull financial data, I built a machine-learning ensemble that projects revenue rising toward ~$18B by 2030 and recommends a $26.5B cumulative marketing plan, split across sponsorships, digital, traditional media and brand IP.

02

The business problem

Problem
Marketing is Red Bull's biggest controllable cost and its core competitive weapon — yet budgeting it five years out, across channels, is mostly guesswork.
My brief
Forecast revenue and the marketing budget it can support through 2030, then translate that into a concrete, channel-level spending roadmap grounded in the company's real financials.
03

Methodology

Build the dataset

Assembled revenue, can-sales and balance-sheet data from 2017–2025 (Kaggle, Yahoo Finance, Google Trends, company filings), converting EUR to USD at historical rates.

Clean with SQL

Used SQLite queries and Pandas to filter, aggregate and remove irrelevant fields before modeling.

Blend three models

Rather than trust one model on limited annual data, I combined Polynomial Regression, Gradient Boosting and Exponential Trend in a weighted ensemble — deliberately downweighting the model that overfit, so the forecast tracked the real macro-trend.

Allocate the budget

Projected the marketing budget as a share of revenue and split it across channels using Red Bull's known spend mix.

05

The revenue forecast

The ensemble projects revenue climbing from about $12.7B in 2025 to ~$18B by 2030 — a steady ~7.3% growth path that anchors every budget decision downstream.

07

Insights & recommendations

STRENGTH

The brand engine works

Sponsorship and experiential marketing drive durable brand equity. Recommendation: protect F1 and extreme-sports spend as the foundation.

OPPORTUNITY

Scale digital faster

Search interest and social engagement are rising. Recommendation: grow the digital share year over year toward 2030.

RISK

Health perception & competition

Energy-drink health concerns and Monster's rising interest are real threats. Recommendation: invest in low-sugar lines and new-market entry.

METHOD NOTE

Why an ensemble

One model fit the history almost perfectly — a classic overfitting trap. Blending three models kept the forecast honest and trend-driven.
